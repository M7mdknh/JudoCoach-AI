# Strategy

## Fighting Taller Opponents
Taller opponents want to establish a high, dominant grip (top of the collar or over the back) to crush your posture and dictate the distance.
*   **Deny the High Grip:** Never let them easily grab the top of your collar. If they get it, break it immediately or duck under it to attack.
*   **Get Underneath:** Their center of gravity is high. Use techniques that drop below their belt line, like Seoi Nage or Drop Tai Otoshi.
*   **Attack the Legs:** Because their legs are long and often spread wide, they are highly vulnerable to inside leg attacks like O Uchi Gari and Ko Uchi Gari.

## Fighting Shorter Opponents
Shorter opponents will naturally try to slip under your center of gravity to execute forward throws like Seoi Nage.
*   **Keep Your Posture Upright:** Do not bend over. Bending over brings your center of gravity closer to theirs and makes their throws easier.
*   **Dominate from the Top:** Use your reach advantage to secure a high collar or back grip. Press your weight down through your grips to make them carry your body weight.
*   **Force Them Backward:** Shorter players hate being driven backward. Use long-reaching attacks like O Soto Gari and Uchi Mata, or snap them down to force a reaction.

## Choosing Techniques for Body Types
Your core throws (Tokui Waza) should match your physical build to maximize leverage and efficiency.
*   **Tall and Lanky:** Focus on throws that use your leg length as a pendulum. *Best choices: Uchi Mata, Harai Goshi, O Soto Gari, and all foot sweeps (De Ashi Barai).*
*   **Short and Stocky:** Focus on throws that require getting completely under the opponent. Your low center of gravity is a massive weapon. *Best choices: Morote Seoi Nage, Ippon Seoi Nage, O Goshi, and Ko Uchi Gari.*
*   **Heavy and Powerful:** Focus on heavy hip throws and sharp rotational throws that use mass to finish the rotation. *Best choices: Tai Otoshi, Harai Goshi, and Koshi Guruma.*

## Grip Fighting (Kumikata)
Grip fighting is not a prelude to Judo; it *is* Judo. If you win the grip fight, you will likely win the match.
*   **Own the Sleeve:** Control the opponent's power hand (sleeve) first. If you control their arm, they cannot turn to throw you.
*   **Inside Position:** Always fight for the inside space. Your hands should ideally be inside their arms, giving you the shortest, strongest path to off-balance them.
*   **Do Not Settle:** If you have a weak grip and they have a strong grip, do not attack. Break their grip, reset, and establish your dominant grips before launching an offensive.

## Preparing for Competition
Competition requires a different mindset than practice. You must be physically peaked and mentally sharp.
*   **Drill Your First Exchange:** Know exactly what grip you want right after the referee says "Hajime." Drill this opening sequence hundreds of times.
*   **Taper Your Training:** Do not do heavy Randori the week of the competition. Keep your heart rate up with light drilling (Uchikomi), but let your muscles recover completely.
*   **Stay Warm:** The hardest part of a tournament is waiting. Once you warm up, keep your gi on and stay moving. Do not sit completely cold for two hours before your match.

## Avoiding Counters
You get countered when you give your opponent an opportunity to take over your movement.
*   **Commit 100%:** The most common reason for being countered is a half-hearted attack. If you decide to enter, you must commit your entire body to the throw. Hesitation gets you thrown.
*   **Maintain Your Pull (Kuzushi):** Counters happen when you stop pulling the sleeve. Keep the tension high on your grips through the entire rotation of the throw.
*   **Keep Your Head Turned:** Where your head looks, your body follows. If you look back at your opponent during a forward throw, you stop your own momentum and invite a counter (like Tani Otoshi).

## Golden Score Strategy
Golden Score is sudden death. Fatigue is high, and mental toughness dictates the winner.
*   **Push the Pace:** The referee is actively looking for stalling. You must maintain a high work rate to force your opponent into making a mistake or taking a Shido (penalty).
*   **Avoid False Attacks:** Do not drop to your knees just to look busy. Referees penalize this heavily in Golden Score. Every attack must have a real setup.
*   **Win the First Grip:** As exhaustion sets in, players get lazy with their hands. Win the gripping exchange immediately off the restart. The fighter who secures the first dominant grip in Golden Score almost always scores the winning point.
