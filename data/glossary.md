# Glossary

## Core Throwing Concepts

### Kuzushi
Off-balancing. This is the most important principle in Judo. It is the act of breaking your opponent's posture so they cannot defend. If you do not have Kuzushi, you are just trying to muscle your opponent over.

### Tsukuri
The entry or "fitting in." This is the movement of stepping into the correct position and loading the opponent onto your body after their balance is broken.

### Kake
The execution or finish of the throw. This is the final phase where the throw is completed and the opponent is driven to the mat.

*Coach's Note: Every successful throw requires all three phases in order: Kuzushi (balance break) -> Tsukuri (entry) -> Kake (execution).*

---

## Roles and Environment

### Tori
The attacker. The person who is executing the technique or throw.

### Uke
The receiver. The person who is being thrown, pinned, or submitted. Being a good Uke is an active skill; you must react realistically and fall safely so your partner can learn.

### Dojo
The training hall or school. In Judo, the Dojo is treated with high respect. You bow when entering and leaving.

### Tatami
The Judo mats. Traditionally made of woven rice straw, modern tatami are made of high-density foam designed to absorb the impact of heavy throws.

### Judogi (or Gi)
The traditional Judo uniform, consisting of a heavy jacket (uwagi), canvas pants (zubon), and a belt (obi). It is heavily reinforced to withstand intense gripping and pulling.

### Obi
The Judo belt. It keeps the jacket closed and indicates the practitioner's rank.

---

## Training Methods

### Randori
Free practice or live sparring. This is where you test your techniques against a fully resisting, unpredictable partner.

### Uchikomi
Repetitive drill training. You practice the Kuzushi (off-balancing) and Tsukuri (entry) phases of a throw repeatedly without actually finishing the throw.

### Nagekomi
Repetitive throwing practice. Usually done onto a thick crash pad, allowing Tori to practice the full execution (Kake) without beating up Uke.

### Ukemi
Breakfalls. The art of falling safely. Proper Ukemi dissipates the energy of a throw and protects your head, neck, and joints from injury. 

---

## Technique Categories

### Tachi-waza
Standing techniques. This encompasses all throwing techniques done from a standing position.

### Ne-waza
Groundwork. This covers all fighting done on the mat, including pins, chokes, and armlocks.

### Osaekomi-waza
Pinning techniques or hold-downs. Holding the opponent largely on their back with control.

### Shime-waza
Choking or strangulation techniques.

### Kansetsu-waza
Joint-locking techniques. In competitive Judo, these are strictly limited to locking the elbow joint.

---

## Match Commands and Scoring

### Hajime
"Begin." The referee's command to start or restart the fight.

### Mate (Mah-tay)
"Wait" or "Stop." The referee's command to pause the match. Fighters must immediately stop and return to their starting positions.

### Sore-made
"That is all." The referee's command indicating the match is over.

### Ippon
A full point. The match ends immediately. Scored by a perfect throw, a 20-second pin, or a submission.

### Waza-ari
A half point. Scored by an imperfect throw or a 10 to 19-second pin. Two Waza-aris equal an Ippon.

### Shido
A minor penalty, usually given for stalling, false attacks, or illegal gripping. Three Shidos result in disqualification.

### Hansoku-make
A major penalty resulting in immediate disqualification, either from accumulating three Shidos or committing a severe rules violation.
