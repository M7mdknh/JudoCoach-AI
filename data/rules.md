# Rules

## Match Duration
A standard competitive Judo match lasts for **4 minutes** of continuous fighting. The clock stops when the referee calls "Mate" (wait) and resumes when they call "Hajime" (begin). 

## Scoring
In Judo, the goal is to win decisively. You win by scoring an Ippon or accumulating two Waza-aris.

### Ippon (Full Point)
An Ippon is a match-ending score. Think of it like a knockout in boxing; as soon as it happens, the match is over. You can score an Ippon in four ways:
*   **Throwing:** Executing a throw with control, speed, and force, making your opponent land largely on their back.
*   **Pinning (Osaekomi):** Holding your opponent down on their back with complete control for **20 seconds**.
*   **Choking (Shime-waza):** Forcing your opponent to tap out or lose consciousness using a legal choke.
*   **Joint Lock (Kansetsu-waza):** Forcing your opponent to tap out using a legal armbar (locking the elbow joint).

### Waza-ari (Half Point)
A Waza-ari is a near-Ippon. If you score two Waza-aris in a single match, they combine to form an Ippon (*Waza-ari-awasete-Ippon*), and the match ends immediately. You score a Waza-ari by:
*   **Throwing:** Executing a throw that lacks one of the required elements of an Ippon (e.g., they land on their side, or the throw lacked sufficient speed or force).
*   **Pinning:** Holding your opponent in a pin for **10 to 19 seconds**.

## Penalties
Judo has strict rules to keep the match active and safe. Penalties force action and punish stalling.

### Shido (Minor Penalty)
A Shido is given for minor rules infractions, usually related to stalling or negative judo. Accumulating three Shidos in a match results in a disqualification. Common reasons for a Shido include:
*   **Passivity:** Not attacking or actively avoiding grips for an extended period.
*   **False Attacks:** Dropping to the mat without a real setup or intention to throw, just to run down the clock or avoid an opponent's grip.
*   **Stepping Out:** Stepping outside the contest area without actively attacking or defending.
*   **Illegal Gripping:** Defensive grip breaking with two hands, holding the belt for too long without attacking, or holding a non-standard grip (like a pistol grip) without an immediate attack.

### Hansoku-make (Disqualification)
A Hansoku-make results in immediate disqualification and loss of the match. It occurs in two ways:
*   **Accumulation:** Receiving your third Shido.
*   **Direct Violation:** Committing a severe or dangerous infraction. 

## Illegal Actions
To protect fighters, certain actions will result in a direct Hansoku-make. These include:
*   **Leg Grabbing:** You cannot grab the opponent's legs or pants with your hands while standing.
*   **Head Diving:** Diving headfirst into the mat while executing a forward throw (like Uchi Mata) to force the rotation. This is extremely dangerous for your neck.
*   **Striking:** Kicking, punching, or any form of striking is strictly forbidden.
*   **Illegal Locks:** Applying joint locks to any joint other than the elbow (e.g., no knee bars, ankle locks, or shoulder locks).
*   **Bending Fingers:** Peeling an opponent's grip by bending their fingers backward.

## Golden Score (Overtime)
If the match ends in a tie (no scores, or equal scores), it goes into Golden Score. 
*   **No Time Limit:** The clock resets, and the match continues until someone wins.
*   **Sudden Death:** The first person to score any point (Waza-ari or Ippon) wins immediately.
*   **Penalties Count:** If a player receives a Shido that brings their total to three, or receives a direct Hansoku-make, their opponent wins.

## Competition Etiquette
Respect is the absolute foundation of Judo. Proper etiquette must be observed at all times on the tatami (mat).
*   **Bowing (Rei):** You must bow when stepping onto the competition mat area, and bow to your opponent before crossing the red danger zone to start the match.
*   **After the Match:** Win or lose, you must return to your starting line, wait for the referee to award the match, bow to your opponent, and step backward respectfully before leaving the mat.
*   **Uniform (Judogi):** Your judogi must be clean, fit properly according to regulation sizing, and be tied neatly before the referee calls "Hajime."
