## O Soto Gari

### Overview
O Soto Gari (Major Outer Reaping) is a fundamental backward throw. You step to the outside of your opponent's legs, break their balance backward, and reap their supporting leg out from under them. It is one of the original 40 throws of Judo.

### Best Situations
* When the opponent is pushing forward aggressively.
* When the opponent stands with a square stance and weight on their heels.
* When you secure a deep, dominant lapel or back grip.

### Avoid Using It When
* The opponent's weight is shifted far forward onto their toes.
* The opponent is heavily pulling your posture down, preventing a deep step.
* The opponent is actively stepping backward away from you.

### Common Mistakes
* Leaving a gap between your chest and the opponent's chest.
* Reaping with a completely straight, stiff leg rather than bending the knee and using a sweeping arc.
* Failing to pull the opponent off balance to their back corner before reaping.

### Coaching Tips
* "Chest to chest!" Drive your shoulder directly into their shoulder to establish control.
* Point the toes of your supporting foot in the direction you want to throw.
* Reap all the way through; imagine your reaping foot touching the back of your own head.

### Common Combinations
* O Uchi Gari to O Soto Gari
* Sasae Tsurikomi Ashi to O Soto Gari
* De Ashi Barai to O Soto Gari

### Suitable Body Types
* Most effective for taller players or those with long legs, though adaptable for all body types with grip modifications.

### Difficulty
* Beginner

### Related Techniques
* O Soto Otoshi
* O Soto Guruma
* O Soto Gake

## Seoi Nage

### Overview
Seoi Nage (Shoulder Throw) is a forward throw where you break the opponent's balance forward, load them onto your back, and throw them over your shoulder. It can be executed with two hands on the jacket (Morote Seoi Nage) or by trapping the arm (Ippon Seoi Nage).

### Best Situations
* When the opponent is pushing forward or actively stepping toward you.
* When the opponent has a high, upright posture.
* When the opponent reaches with a stiff, straight arm.

### Avoid Using It When
* The opponent has established a dominant, high collar grip and is stiff-arming to keep distance.
* The opponent is crouched low in a defensive posture (jigotai).
* You are off-balance backward.

### Common Mistakes
* Pulling the opponent off to the side rather than straight over the top.
* Not dropping your hips low enough beneath the opponent's center of gravity.
* Turning too slowly, allowing the opponent time to drop their weight or step around.

### Coaching Tips
* "Drop below their belt line." Your hips must be lower than theirs to load them effortlessly.
* Look at your "watch" on the pulling hand to generate upward and forward off-balancing (kuzushi).
* Keep your knees bent when loading; do not stand up straight until the execution phase.

### Common Combinations
* Ko Uchi Gari to Seoi Nage
* O Uchi Gari to Seoi Nage
* Kouchi Makikomi to Seoi Nage

### Suitable Body Types
* Exceptionally suited for shorter players throwing taller opponents, allowing them to naturally get under the center of gravity.

### Difficulty
* Beginner to Intermediate

### Related Techniques
* Ippon Seoi Nage
* Morote Seoi Nage
* Seoi Otoshi

### Recommended Videos
* [Kodokan: Seoi-nage](https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=Kodokan+Seoi+Nage)

## Uchi Mata

### Overview
Uchi Mata (Inner Thigh Throw) is a powerful forward throw utilizing a leg sweep against the opponent's inner thigh, launching them forward and over your back. It is one of the highest-scoring throws in competitive Judo.

### Best Situations
* When the opponent is in a bent-over posture or heavily defending their lapel.
* When the opponent is circling away or pressing forward heavily.
* When you have secured a high, dominant collar or back grip.

### Avoid Using It When
* You are off-balance backward.
* The opponent has successfully broken your posture down, bending your spine.
* The opponent has a wider-than-shoulder-width defensive stance that prevents leg entry.

### Common Mistakes
* Reaping too early before properly breaking the opponent's balance forward.
* Keeping the sweeping leg completely straight; it should have a slight, dynamic bend.
* Failing to pull the opponent tight to the chest, leaving a gap that destroys leverage.

### Coaching Tips
* "Pull them into the space you create." Make them float before the leg enters.
* Use a pendulum motion: as your upper body goes down, your sweeping leg goes up.
* Look in the direction of the throw to turn your shoulders and follow through.

### Common Combinations
* O Uchi Gari to Uchi Mata
* Ko Uchi Gari to Uchi Mata
* Tai Otoshi to Uchi Mata

### Suitable Body Types
* Excellent for taller players with long legs, providing massive leverage.

### Difficulty
* Advanced

### Related Techniques
* Hane Goshi
* Harai Goshi
* Ashi Uchi Mata

### Recommended Videos
* [Kodokan: Uchi-mata](https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=Kodokan+Uchi+Mata)

## Tai Otoshi

### Overview
Tai Otoshi (Body Drop) is a rapid hand throw where you drop your hips, extend a blocking leg across the opponent's path, and use a powerful rotational pull to throw them over the extended leg.

### Best Situations
* When the opponent is stepping forward or circling around you.
* When the opponent's weight is heavily committed to their toes.
* When the opponent defends a hip throw by dropping their weight.

### Avoid Using It When
* The opponent is leaning far backward.
* The opponent has fully secured a dominant grip that prevents you from turning your shoulders.
* The opponent's feet are completely static and heavy.

### Common Mistakes
* Putting too much weight on the blocking leg (it should be light and easily adjustable).
* Bending the blocking leg deeply, turning the move into a hip throw.
* Pulling downward instead of pulling out and rotating in a circular motion.

### Coaching Tips
* "Snap the steering wheel." This is a hand throw, not a leg sweep. Fast, sharp hands are key.
* Your blocking leg is just a tripping hazard, not a weight-bearing pillar. Keep it springy.
* Maintain an upright torso during the rotation.

### Common Combinations
* O Uchi Gari to Tai Otoshi
* Sasae Tsurikomi Ashi to Tai Otoshi
* Seoi Nage to Tai Otoshi

### Suitable Body Types
* Highly effective for all body types, but exceptional for players with fast hands and good rotational speed.

### Difficulty
* Intermediate

### Related Techniques
* Seoi Nage
* Uki Otoshi
* Ashi Guruma

### Recommended Videos
* [Kodokan: Tai-otoshi](https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=Kodokan+Tai+Otoshi)

## Harai Goshi

### Overview
Harai Goshi (Sweeping Hip Throw) is a hip throw where you break the opponent's balance forward, load them onto your hip, and sweep their legs away using your own leg.

### Best Situations
* When the opponent is defending a standard hip throw by stepping their lead leg to the outside.
* When the opponent is moving laterally.
* From a strong underhook, back grip, or deep collar grip.

### Avoid Using It When
* The opponent has a very low center of gravity and is actively squatting down defensively.
* The opponent has a strong stiff-arm preventing you from getting hip-to-hip contact.

### Common Mistakes
* Sweeping the leg before establishing tight hip contact.
* Turning the head away from the direction of the throw.
* Sweeping with a bent knee instead of generating power from the hip with a straight leg.

### Coaching Tips
* "Load the spring." Secure tight hip contact before sweeping; the hip throws, the leg just finishes.
* The sweeping leg should brush the mat as it comes up, like a broom sweeping dust.
* Pull the sleeve hand tightly to your own chest to prevent the opponent from slipping off.

### Common Combinations
* O Soto Gari to Harai Goshi
* Sasae Tsurikomi Ashi to Harai Goshi
* O Uchi Gari to Harai Goshi

### Suitable Body Types
* Excellent for heavier or taller players with powerful hips and a strong core.

### Difficulty
* Intermediate

### Related Techniques
* Uki Goshi
* O Goshi
* Uchi Mata

### Recommended Videos
* [Kodokan: Harai-goshi](https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=Kodokan+Harai+Goshi)

## O Uchi Gari

### Overview
O Uchi Gari (Major Inner Reaping) is a backward throw where you step deeply between the opponent's legs and use your leg to reap their inside leg in a large circular motion, driving them straight backward.

### Best Situations
* When the opponent stands with a wide base or a defensive square stance.
* When the opponent is leaning backward on their heels.
* When you want to force a reaction to set up a forward throw.

### Avoid Using It When
* The opponent is lunging forward heavily.
* The opponent is moving rapidly backward out of your sweeping range.

### Common Mistakes
* Reaching with the reaping leg without committing the hips and stepping in deeply.
* Pulling the opponent forward instead of driving their weight backward over their heels.
* Reaping straight back instead of in a large semi-circle.

### Coaching Tips
* "Drive the steering wheel down and back." Your chest must drive them backward as your leg hooks.
* Draw a big circle with your big toe on the mat when you reap.
* Keep your head up and chest out to maintain driving pressure.

### Common Combinations
* O Uchi Gari to Seoi Nage
* O Uchi Gari to Uchi Mata
* O Uchi Gari to Tai Otoshi

### Suitable Body Types
* Universally effective for all body types; a critical setup technique for every competitive Judoka.

### Difficulty
* Beginner

### Related Techniques
* Ko Uchi Gari
* O Soto Gari
* O Uchi Gaeshi

### Recommended Videos
* [Kodokan: O-uchi-gari](https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=Kodokan+O+Uchi+Gari)

## Ko Uchi Gari

### Overview
Ko Uchi Gari (Minor Inner Reaping) is a foot sweep where you sweep the heel of the opponent's advancing foot from the inside just as they place their weight onto it, dropping them backward.

### Best Situations
* When the opponent is stepping forward towards you.
* To break a static, defensive posture and force the opponent to move.
* When the opponent stands with a staggered stance, exposing the lead leg.

### Avoid Using It When
* The opponent's weight is entirely on their back foot, making the front foot too light and easy to lift.
* You are standing too far away to reach without leaning over and breaking your posture.

### Common Mistakes
* Kicking the leg instead of sweeping the heel along the mat.
* Bending forward at the waist and breaking your own posture, which invites counter-attacks.
* Sweeping too early or too late, missing the precise moment of weight transfer.

### Coaching Tips
* "Sweep the sole of their foot." Cup the back of their heel with the bottom of your foot.
* Time the sweep exactly when their foot is planting—like pulling a rug out from under them.
* Drive with your hands simultaneously to push them over the swept leg.

### Common Combinations
* Ko Uchi Gari to Seoi Nage
* Ko Uchi Gari to O Uchi Gari
* Ko Uchi Gari to Uchi Mata

### Suitable Body Types
* Excellent for shorter players and those with quick, agile footwork.

### Difficulty
* Intermediate (The mechanics are simple, but live timing is very difficult).

### Related Techniques
* De Ashi Barai
* O Uchi Gari
* Kouchi Makikomi

### Recommended Videos
* [Kodokan: Ko-uchi-gari](https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=Kodokan+Ko+Uchi+Gari)

## De Ashi Barai

### Overview
De Ashi Barai (Advanced Foot Sweep) is a timing-based throw targeting the opponent's advancing foot just before it bears full weight, sweeping it completely across their body to the outside.

### Best Situations
* When the opponent is walking forward, stepping backward, or stepping sideways.
* During gripping exchanges when the opponent is heavily focused on the upper body and neglecting their feet.

### Avoid Using It When
* The opponent is perfectly still, flat-footed, and heavily rooted to the tatami.
* The opponent's weight is already fully committed to the target leg.

### Common Mistakes
* Sweeping with a stiff, rigid leg.
* Looking down at the feet instead of maintaining an upright posture and looking forward.
* Failing to use the hands to coordinate the off-balancing (kuzushi) with the foot sweep.

### Coaching Tips
* "Hands and feet move together." As your foot sweeps, your sleeve hand pulls down in a steering wheel motion.
* Turn your sweeping foot sideways so the bottom of your foot cups their ankle.
* Sweep lightly and smoothly; it relies entirely on timing, not brute force.

### Common Combinations
* De Ashi Barai to Tai Otoshi
* De Ashi Barai to O Soto Gari
* De Ashi Barai to Harai Goshi

### Suitable Body Types
* All body types, but heavily favors those with natural rhythm, good timing, and relaxed movement.

### Difficulty
* Beginner to Intermediate (Mechanics are easy to learn, live timing is hard to master).

### Related Techniques
* Okuri Ashi Barai
* Harai Tsurikomi Ashi
* Tsubame Gaeshi

### Recommended Videos
* [Kodokan: De-ashi-harai](https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=Kodokan+De+Ashi+Barai)

## Sasae Tsurikomi Ashi

### Overview
Sasae Tsurikomi Ashi (Propping and Drawing Ankle Throw) is a forward throw where you block the opponent's advancing ankle and use a massive lifting and pulling motion to rotate them over the blocked leg.

### Best Situations
* When the opponent is moving in a circle.
* When the opponent is pressing their weight heavily onto their front foot.
* When the opponent pushes against you to defend a backward throw.

### Avoid Using It When
* The opponent is actively pulling away or stepping backward.
* You do not have a strong grip on the lapel or sleeve to perform the drawing action.

### Common Mistakes
* Kicking the ankle instead of firmly propping your foot against it.
* Failing to lift with the lapel hand (the "tsurikomi" action) to bring the opponent onto their toes.
* Turning the shoulders without shifting the hips, killing rotational power.

### Coaching Tips
* "Block, don't kick." Your foot acts as a static hurdle, not a weapon.
* Imagine throwing the opponent into a trash can located right next to your blocking foot.
* Rotate your upper body sharply like a steering wheel to snap them over the block.

### Common Combinations
* Sasae Tsurikomi Ashi to O Soto Gari
* Sasae Tsurikomi Ashi to Tai Otoshi
* Sasae Tsurikomi Ashi to Harai Goshi

### Suitable Body Types
* Highly effective for all body types.

### Difficulty
* Intermediate

### Related Techniques
* Hiza Guruma
* Harai Tsurikomi Ashi
* De Ashi Barai
